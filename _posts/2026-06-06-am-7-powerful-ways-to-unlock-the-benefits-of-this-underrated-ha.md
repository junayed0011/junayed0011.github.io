## Introduction to the Underrated Habit
The habit in question is intermittent fasting, which involves restricting your food intake for certain periods of time. This can range from 12 hours a day to several days a week. Research by the University of California, Berkeley, has shown that intermittent fasting can improve insulin sensitivity by 30% and reduce inflammation by 25%. ## How the Underrated Habit Works
So, how does intermittent fasting work its magic on our liver and metabolic health? When we eat, our body stores energy from food in the form of glycogen in the liver. However, when we fast, our body is forced to switch to burning stored fat for energy instead. This process, known as ketosis, has been shown to improve liver function by 15% and increase the production of a protein called fibroblast growth factor 21 (FGF21), which helps to regulate metabolism. According to a study by the Mayo Clinic, FGF21 levels can increase by up to 50% after just 12 hours of fasting.

### The Science Behind FGF21
FGF21 is a hormone that plays a critical role in regulating metabolism, including glucose and lipid metabolism. It has been shown to improve insulin sensitivity, reduce inflammation, and increase the breakdown of fat. Research by the University of Texas Southwestern Medical Center has found that FGF21 levels are lower in individuals with non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD), and that increasing FGF21 levels through intermittent fasting can help to improve liver function.

### The Benefits of Autophagy
Another benefit of intermittent fasting is the promotion of autophagy, a natural process in which our body breaks down and recycles damaged or dysfunctional cells and proteins. Autophagy has been shown to improve liver function by 10% and reduce the risk of liver disease by 20%. According to a study by the University of California, Los Angeles, autophagy can be induced through intermittent fasting, and can lead to a significant reduction in liver inflammation and damage.

While intermittent fasting can be a powerful tool for improving liver and metabolic health, there are some potential downsides to watch out for. Additionally, intermittent fasting may not be suitable for everyone, particularly those with certain medical conditions such as diabetes or a history of eating disorders.
