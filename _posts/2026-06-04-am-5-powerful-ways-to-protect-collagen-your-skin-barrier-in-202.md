## Understanding the Importance of Collagen and Skin Barrier
Collagen is the most abundant protein in the human body, accounting for about 25% to 35% of all proteins. It plays a vital role in maintaining skin elasticity, firmness, and hydration. The skin barrier, on the other hand, is the outermost layer of the skin that protects it from external stressors. A study conducted by the National Institute of Environmental Health Sciences found that exposure to UV radiation can lead to a 20% decrease in collagen production. Furthermore, research by the cosmetics company, L'Oréal, has shown that a damaged skin barrier can lead to a 30% increase in water loss, resulting in dry and irritated skin.

## Adopting an Inside-Out Approach
To protect your skin barrier and collagen reserves, it's essential to adopt an inside-out approach that includes a combination of dietary changes, lifestyle modifications, and topical treatments. Here are 5 science-backed habits to include in your skin longevity guide:
