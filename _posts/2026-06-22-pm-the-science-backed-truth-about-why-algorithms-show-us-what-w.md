## Introduction to Algorithmic Decision-Making
Algorithms are designed to learn from our behavior and provide us with content that is likely to engage us. However, this process can lead to a phenomenon known as the "filter bubble," where we are shown only what the algorithm thinks we want to see, rather than what we actually need or want. A study by the University of California, Berkeley found that 62% of adults in the United States have noticed that their social media feeds are tailored to their interests, but 45% of these individuals also reported feeling frustrated by the lack of diversity in their feeds.

### Strategies for Avoiding the Filter Bubble
There are several strategies that can help us avoid the filter bubble and ensure that we are shown a diverse range of content. These include:
1. Seeking out multiple sources of information
2. Engaging with content that challenges our assumptions
3. Using alternative platforms or browsers that do not track our behavior
4. Regularly clearing our browser history and cookies
5. Using ad blockers or other tools to limit the amount of personalized advertising we see

Some additional tips for avoiding the filter bubble include:
* Using social media platforms that prioritize diversity and inclusivity
* Following accounts or pages that promote diverse perspectives and opinions
* Engaging with content that is outside of our usual interests or comfort zone
* Supporting independent media outlets and journalists who prioritize fact-based reporting
