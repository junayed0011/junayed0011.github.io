## Introduction to Healthcare ETFs
Healthcare ETFs offer a diversified portfolio of stocks from various healthcare sectors, including pharmaceuticals, biotechnology, and medical devices. Vanguard (VHT) and VanEck (PPH) are two of the most well-established healthcare ETFs, with a track record of delivering strong returns to investors. However, with different investment strategies and holdings, it's essential to evaluate which one is the better buy.

## Investment Strategy and Holdings
Vanguard (VHT) follows a market-cap-weighted index, tracking the performance of the CRSP US Healthcare Index. This index comprises over 300 stocks from various healthcare sectors, with the top holdings including Johnson & Johnson, Pfizer, and UnitedHealth Group. In contrast, VanEck (PPH) uses a rules-based approach, selecting stocks from the NASDAQ OMX Global Healthcare Index. This index focuses on pharmaceutical and biotechnology companies, with top holdings including Amgen, Gilead Sciences, and Biogen.

### Vanguard (VHT) Performance
Vanguard (VHT) has delivered a 5-year average annual return of 12.1%, outperforming the S&P 500 index by 2.5%. The ETF has a low expense ratio of 0.10% and a dividend yield of 1.2%. According to a study by Morningstar, Vanguard (VHT) has consistently ranked among the top-performing healthcare ETFs, with a 10-year return of 14.5%.

### VanEck (PPH) Performance
VanEck (PPH) has delivered a 5-year average annual return of 10.5%, underperforming the S&P 500 index by 1.1%. The ETF has a higher expense ratio of 0.35% and a dividend yield of 0.5%. However, VanEck (PPH) has shown resilience during market downturns, with a beta of 0.8 compared to the S&P 500 index.

## What to Watch Out For
When evaluating Vanguard (VHT) vs VanEck (PPH), it's essential to consider the following factors:
1. **Expense ratio**: Vanguard (VHT) has a significantly lower expense ratio, which can result in higher net returns for investors.
2. **Investment strategy**: VanEck (PPH) focuses on pharmaceutical and biotechnology companies, which may be more volatile than the diversified portfolio of Vanguard (VHT).
3. **Dividend yield**: Vanguard (VHT) offers a higher dividend yield, which may appeal to income-seeking investors.

Some common misconceptions about healthcare ETFs include:
* Assuming that all healthcare ETFs are created equal, when in fact they have different investment strategies and holdings.
* Overlooking the importance of expense ratios, which can significantly impact net returns.
