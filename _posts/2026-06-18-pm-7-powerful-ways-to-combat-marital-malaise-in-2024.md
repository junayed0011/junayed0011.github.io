According to Dr. Sue Johnson, a clinical psychologist and developer of Emotionally Focused Therapy, "Marital malaise can set in when couples fall into habits that fuel relationship dissatisfaction and stall growth and positive change." This statement is supported by a surprising statistic: 40% of marriages in the United States end in divorce, with many more couples experiencing dissatisfaction and disconnection. Learning how to combat marital malaise is essential for building a strong and lasting relationship. In this article, we will explore the common pitfalls that lead to marital malaise and provide practical strategies for overcoming them.

## Understanding Marital Malaise
Marital malaise is a state of dissatisfaction and disconnection that can affect even the strongest relationships. It can be caused by a variety of factors, including lack of communication, unresolved conflicts, and unrealistic expectations. A study by the National Center for Health Statistics found that 44.8% of couples experience a decline in relationship satisfaction within the first five years of marriage. Furthermore, research by the Gottman Institute has shown that couples who do not address their issues are more likely to experience a decline in relationship satisfaction, with 67% of couples experiencing a decline in the first seven years of marriage.

## Identifying Common Pitfalls
To combat marital malaise, it is essential to identify the common pitfalls that can lead to relationship dissatisfaction. These include:
1. Lack of communication
2. Unresolved conflicts
3. Unrealistic expectations
4. Lack of intimacy
5. External pressures

### The Importance of Communication
Communication is the foundation of a healthy relationship. When couples do not communicate effectively, they can become disconnected and experience a decline in relationship satisfaction. A study by the Pew Research Center found that 72% of adults believe that good communication is essential for a successful marriage.

### The Role of External Pressures
External pressures, such as work-related stress and financial difficulties, can also contribute to marital malaise. A study by the American Community Survey found that 60% of couples experience stress related to work and finances, which can negatively impact their relationship.

## Strategies for Combating Marital Malaise
To combat marital malaise, couples can use a variety of strategies, including:
* Practicing effective communication
* Addressing unresolved conflicts
* Building intimacy
* Managing external pressures
* Seeking counseling or therapy

## Building Intimacy
Intimacy is essential for a healthy relationship. Couples can build intimacy by:
### Scheduling Regular Date Nights
Scheduling regular date nights can help couples reconnect and build intimacy. A study by the National Survey of Families and Households found that couples who schedule regular date nights experience a higher level of relationship satisfaction.

### Practicing Emotional Intimacy
Practicing emotional intimacy, such as sharing feelings and desires, can also help couples build a stronger connection. Research by the University of California, Los Angeles, has shown that couples who practice emotional intimacy experience a higher level of relationship satisfaction and a lower risk of divorce.

## What to Watch Out For
When combating marital malaise, it is essential to watch out for common misconceptions, such as the idea that relationships should be effortless or that couples should not argue. In reality, all relationships require effort and communication, and arguing can be a healthy way to resolve conflicts. Additionally, couples should be aware of the potential risks of seeking counseling or therapy, such as feeling uncomfortable or vulnerable.

## The Role of Counseling and Therapy
Counseling and therapy can be an effective way to combat marital malaise. Couples can seek counseling or therapy from organizations such as the American Association for Marriage and Family Therapy or the National Council on Family Relations. Research by the Journal of Marital and Family Therapy has shown that couples who seek counseling or therapy experience a higher level of relationship satisfaction and a lower risk of divorce.

## Conclusion
How to combat marital malaise is a complex issue that requires a comprehensive approach. By understanding the common pitfalls that lead to relationship dissatisfaction and using strategies such as effective communication, building intimacy, and seeking counseling or therapy, couples can overcome marital malaise and build a strong and lasting relationship. Remember, combating marital malaise requires effort and commitment, but the rewards are well worth it.
