## Resolving Cofounder Conflicts
Resolving cofounder conflicts requires a structured approach. Here are 7 proven ways to resolve cofounder conflicts:
1. Establish clear communication channels
2. Define roles and responsibilities
3. Set clear goals and expectations
4. Practice active listening
5. Address conflicts promptly
6. Seek external help when needed
7. Foster a culture of trust and respect

Some key strategies for resolving cofounder conflicts include:
* Regularly scheduling meetings to discuss challenges and goals
* Establishing a clear decision-making process
* Encouraging open and honest communication
* Fostering a culture of trust and respect
* Seeking external help when needed, such as a business coach or therapist

## What to Watch Out For
When resolving cofounder conflicts, there are several common misconceptions to watch out for. One common misconception is that cofounder conflicts can be resolved by simply "talking it out." While communication is essential, it's not always enough to resolve deep-seated conflicts. Another misconception is that cofounder conflicts are a sign of weakness. In reality, conflicts can be a sign of strength, as they indicate that cofounders are passionate about their business and willing to work through challenges.

## The Role of Psychology in Cofounder Conflict
Research by the University of Michigan shows that psychology plays a significant role in cofounder conflict. Cofounders with different personality traits, such as introversion and extroversion, can experience conflicts due to their differing work styles. Additionally, cofounders with different attachment styles, such as anxious or avoidant, can experience conflicts due to their differing emotional needs. For example, the cofounders of Google, Larry Page and Sergey Brin, have spoken about how their different personality traits and work styles have sometimes led to conflicts, but also driven innovation and growth.
